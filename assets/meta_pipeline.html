The Meta-Loop: a general model of a self-tuning process

This is not a description of a pipeline. A pipeline is just one revolution of the cycle; the real object is the improvement loop. A product = a set of independent self-tuning loops; the LLM inside them is secondary and replaceable, the architecture remains.

Genesis of a loop: where the roles come from

Every process starts as human work → a temporary acceptance worker appears → accumulated decisions turn into a deterministic process → the worker is no longer needed → the director stops intervening in operations. A loop is mature when its recurring decisions are fully expressed in code and rules. Every role is technical debt: it exists only while the process is not yet mature enough.
  • The law of decision gravity: every decision tends to sink one level down — director → hypothesis → rule → code → conveyor. A month later the director has forgotten that category of decisions exists.
  • The seniority law: the director's actions are terminal for every automated role — no worker, manager, or builder may revert, overwrite, or re-decide them: not in data, not in code, not in structure.
  • The denominator law: a metric may never improve by shrinking what it measures (sweep samples, reviewer caps, rate denominators); coverage cuts follow confirmed quality, never produce it.
  • The main flow is not actions but knowledge: the loop turns complaints and verdicts into rules, rules into code.
  • The director is not temporary. He stops running operations — but he alone changes the goals.
  • Complaints outweigh any internal metric — an external source of truth; without it the loop optimizes its own grades.
▼ roles of one revolution

1 · CONVEYOR — the work process determinism + local models

  • Ships the entire incoming window, leaving nothing undecided: every item gets exactly one journaled fate, and the journal partitions add up — "unsure" is itself a fate (parked, visibly, until evidence arrives), never a silent leftover
  • Every action reversible; runs on a schedule, lives locally, costs little
  • The run journal is also its clock: every record carries mode (live/dry) and aborted; only successful live runs advance the intake window — rehearsals and crashes never eat material
Contract: every decision → journal (what, why, which signals); caps on volume per run; engine edits only cold — freeze, accumulate, one deploy behind a pre-flight checklist (syntax → tests → rehearsal → checksums), unfreeze.
▼ the full delivery, already shelved

2 · ACCEPTANCE WORKER — temporary inspector LLM (being phased out)

  • Full post-shipment acceptance sweep — independently re-checks the entire delivery, outside the hot path: lost? handled correctly? right shelf? a one-off or a class of failure?
  • Any conveyor mistake is his to fix — reversibly, with an undo journal; a wrongly-shelved item goes back through the conveyor rather than being hand-placed
  • Bounded by default: under doubt the verdict is no-op (leave it, never improve it); corrections per run are hard-capped — a sweep that "finds" connections manufactures them
  • New powers ship in would-mode behind a write-guard: journaled "would have fixed" until the dry record earns the director's flag; the director's own verdicts are untouchable
  • Unsure → the director's doubt queue; systematic patterns → escalated straight to the manager
Contract: verdict + reason → journal, every run; zero-cost exit when the delivery is empty; the no-op share of the sweep is the loop's headline maturity metric; a stronger (more expensive) model here is legitimate — a sharper inspector retires himself sooner; exists until the conveyor learns to do it itself.
▼ applied fixes + doubts  ·  systemic escalations ⤵ straight to the manager (4)

3 · STOREFRONT + COMPLAINTS director (owner)

  • The result works immediately (goods on the shelves); the director need not approve anything in advance
  • Complaint: one undo action + a free-text "why this was wrong" — the highest ground truth
  • His feedback is its own channel: a verdict table in the node's store, applied at the start of every live run; a director verdict outranks any sweep verdict and is never deleted — it feeds the panel and the manager
  • Observation panel: live quality by tier, an action feed with reasons, liveness markers, the doubt queue
Contract: silence = consent; complaints outweigh any internal metric; the panel has a deterministic self-test — a gauge that disagrees with reality is repaired as its own class of work; everything visible in one place in 5 minutes.
▼ journals + panel & trend + complaints + the worker's escalations ▼

4 · OPTIMIZATION MANAGER — analysis and decisions strong LLM

  • Breakdown: the conveyor's work in numbers; conveyor problems and worker problems — kept separate; trend against past reviews; a sample audit with its own eyes — samples disagreeing with the panel indict the metric, not the conveyor
  • Triage of the worker's escalations — mandatory, each one: real? fix at all? fix differently? already carded?
  • One increment in measurement at a time (WIP=1): every solution declares a metric_target and its own closing condition (measure_after: runs / director verdicts / days — whichever count is reached first, with a timeout safety net); while the window is open the manager exits without an LLM call
  • Window closes → verdict: accept / rollback / inconclusive, grounded in the frozen baseline vs the panel
  • Ladder of alternatives before any tune-up: a plain heuristic → a cheaper/local model → a wider redesign; changes that alter how the conveyor decides must pass the rehearsal gate — replay over recorded history against what actually happened
  • Escalations to the director: product forks, trade-offs, dead-ends; anything it can test itself it decides and files as an FYI. Once a day — a brief upward: verdicts closed, escalations, FYIs, in one digest
Contract: honors the directives file (a numbered two-way protocol, re-read every cycle); decisions are general rules (properties and thresholds, never hardcoded names — a spot fix is blocked, not built); a solution without a metric_target is dropped deterministically; decisions → cards in a shared backlog, one label per team.
▼ auto-chain: the top decision goes straight to building

5 · BUILDER — implementation strong LLM, agentic

  • Takes only cards with its own label → minimal diff + unit test + docs in the same pass → mandatory eval gate (held-out metrics no worse than baseline)
  • Gate passed → the change goes live for the next conveyor run; failure → automatic revert and an honest "why"
  • Never reverts the director: a change that undoes his verdicts — in code, data, or structure — is refused
Contract: edits only its own process files (whitelist); backup before editing; a blocked build sheds the builder's label and gains the escalation label — the loop does not hammer the same wall; chain and daily caps bound autonomy; result goes to the director for acceptance (only he marks Done).
⟳ loop closed: the conveyor got better → less work for the worker → fewer complaints for the director

👔 Director: two controls

  • Complaints — pointed corrections of facts ("this doesn't go there, because…")
  • Directives — the vector for the manager; starts as one line, grows into a numbered two-way protocol: manager escalates a fork → the answer is recorded as a directive → that category never comes back
  • Acceptance: Review → Done; escalations require his answer

📏 Loop maturity criteria

  • Acceptance no-op rate ≥ 95–98% — the headline metric: the sweep changes nothing because the conveyor already shelved everything; below that, every non-ok verdict is the manager's pre-labeled work queue
  • Share of decisions without a cloud LLM → rising; complaints → toward zero; director involvement ≈ 5 min/day
  • Cost falls only as a consequence of the no-op rate rising — never as a target (the denominator law)
  • Removing control tiers — by numbers only, bottom-up

🌱 Shelves are born and die

  • Birth needs an evidence bar: below the declared threshold the candidate is parked, visibly, awaiting evidence — a fate, not a rejection
  • The dead don't crowd the living: dedup runs only against live shelves; a retired shelf leaves an immutable tombstone that never blocks a new birth
  • Rebirth is a road, not a resurrection: a wrongly killed shelf returns through the normal birth path — never by editing the tombstone

🔩 Reliability contracts

  • Journals are append-only — a correction is a new line; baseline and verdicts replay from frozen history
  • Zero-cost empty path for every worker: no LLM call, no journal noise when there is no work
  • One writer at a time: deploys serialized; a store shared by two loops has a single writer
  • Instruments are code too: the panel self-tests; a lying gauge is fixed before the conveyor is tuned
